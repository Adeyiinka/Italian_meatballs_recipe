Recipe Name - Tender Italian meatballs

Description

Tender Italian meatballs are described as soft, light, juicy, and flavorful, often having a "melt-in-your-mouth" or "pillowy soft" texture. They are typically made with a blend of meats and other ingredients that enhance moisture and prevent them from becoming dense.

Recipe details

Prep Time
15 to 25 minutes
Cook Time
45 minutes to over 2 hours (depending on simmering time)
Servings
4 to 6 servings (makes approximately 24-28 1.5-inch meatballs)
Difficulty
Easy

Ingredients list

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (80-85% lean)
  • 1 lb (450g) ground pork (or use a mix of beef, pork, and veal)
  • 1 cup fresh breadcrumbs (from 2-3 slices of white bread)
  • ½ cup milk (whole or 2%)
  • ½ cup freshly grated Parmigiano Reggiano or Pecorino Romano cheese, plus more for serving
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • ¼ cup fresh Italian flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely minced
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil, for frying
  • 1 (24 oz) jar or can of your favorite tomato passata or crushed tomatoes

Instructions

  1. Soak the bread: In a small bowl, tear the bread into small pieces and cover with milk. Let it soak for about 5-10 minutes until completely moist, then mash with a fork to form a coarse paste (a panade).
  2. Combine ingredients: In a large bowl, add the ground beef and pork. Add the mashed bread mixture, beaten eggs, Parmesan cheese, parsley, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Use your hands to gently mix all the ingredients until just combined. Do not overmix, as this can make the meatballs tough.
  3. Shape the meatballs: With wet or lightly oiled hands, roll the mixture into 1.5 to 2-inch (golf ball size) rounds. This recipe typically yields about 20-24 meatballs.
  4. Brown the meatballs: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the meatballs in a single layer, in batches if necessary to avoid overcrowding the pan. Fry, turning occasionally, until nicely browned on all sides (about 5-10 minutes).
  5. Simmer in sauce: While the meatballs are browning, heat your tomato sauce in a separate large pot or a large, deep pan. Once all the meatballs are browned, gently transfer them into the simmering sauce. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let them simmer gently for at least 30 minutes, or longer for a richer flavor, until cooked through.
  6. Serve: Serve the meatballs hot with your favorite pasta or crusty Italian bread, garnished with extra fresh basil and grated Parmesan cheese.

Notes

The key to tender, flavorful Italian meatballs lies in using a bread and milk mixture (panade) to lock in moisture and carefully avoiding overmixing the meat. Browning them first, then simmering them gently in a rich tomato sauce, ensures the best results. This is a link to the recipe source.
